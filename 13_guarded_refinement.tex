\section{Guarded Empirical Refinement}
\label{sec:main-guard}

Population value must be estimated before a runtime learner commits a new distinction. For an atom $B$ and candidate split $g:B\to\{0,1\}$, the empirical log-loss improvement is
\begin{align*}
\widehat V_{\log}(B,g)
=
H(\widehat p_B)
-
\sum_{b\in\{0,1\}}
\frac{|B_b|}{|B|}H(\widehat p_{B_b}),
\qquad
B_b=\{i\in B:g(x_i)=b\}.
\end{align*}
The analogous squared-loss score is $\operatorname{SSE}(B)-\operatorname{SSE}(B_0)-\operatorname{SSE}(B_1)$.

For a state $S$ and held-out scoring buffer $\calB_t^{\rm score}$ of size $m_t$, define
\begin{align*}
\widehat R(S;\calB_t^{\rm score})
=
\frac1{m_t}\sum_{(\omega_i,Y_i)\in\calB_t^{\rm score}}
L(Y_i,f_S(\omega_i)),
\qquad
\widehat G_t(S')
=
\widehat R(S_t;\calB_t^{\rm score})
-
\widehat R(S';\calB_t^{\rm score}).
\end{align*}
Let $\calC_t^{\rm rep}$ be repair candidates and $\calC_t^{\rm ref}$ refinement candidates. Proposal may use a disjoint buffer, but the scoring buffer must be held out from proposal and previous state-selection decisions. The identity state is included among repair candidates, so a harmful local update can be declined.

\begin{theorem}[Guarded empirical refinement, informal version of Theorem~\ref{thm:safe-refinement:formal}]
\label{thm:main-guarded-refinement}
Assume losses lie in $[0,1]$. At time $t$, let $\calC_t=\calC_t^{\rm rep}\cup\calC_t^{\rm ref}$ be predictable from the past and proposal buffer, and let $\calB_t^{\rm score}$ contain $m_t$ fresh held-out samples independent of candidate selection. Define
\begin{align*}
\epsilon_t
=
\sqrt{
\frac{
\log\bigl(4t^2(|\calC_t|+1)/\delta\bigr)
}{
2m_t
}
}.
\end{align*}
Then with probability at least $1-\delta$, uniformly over all $t$ and $S'\in\calC_t$,
\begin{align*}
\left|\widehat G_t(S')-G_t(S')\right|
\le 2\epsilon_t.
\end{align*}
Consequently, if a refinement $S_f$ is accepted because
\begin{align*}
\widehat G_t(S_f)-\lambda c(S_f)
>
\widehat G_t(S_r)+\tau+4\epsilon_t,
\end{align*}
where $S_r$ is the best empirical repair candidate, then
\begin{align*}
G_t(S_f)-\lambda c(S_f)
>
G_t(S_r)+\tau.
\end{align*}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Condition on the past filtration. The old state and all candidate states are fixed. Hoeffding and a union bound give, uniformly over the old state and $S'\in\calC_t$,
\begin{align*}
\left|\widehat R(S')-R(S')\right|
\le \epsilon_t.
\end{align*}
Since a gain is a difference of two risks,
\begin{align*}
\left|\widehat G_t(S')-G_t(S')\right|
\le 2\epsilon_t.
\end{align*}
On this event,
\begin{align*}
G_t(S_f)-\lambda c(S_f)
&\ge \widehat G_t(S_f)-\lambda c(S_f)-2\epsilon_t\\
&> \widehat G_t(S_r)+\tau+2\epsilon_t\\
&\ge G_t(S_r)+\tau.
\end{align*}
The $t^{-2}$ schedule makes the bound simultaneous over all times.
\end{proof}

The theorem is a finite-sample guard, not a global training theorem. The $4\epsilon_t$ term accounts for sampling uncertainty, while $\tau$ is a modeling margin for unmodeled costs or a desired minimum effect size. If the same validation buffer is reused adaptively, one needs reusable-holdout or adaptive-validation control. Appendix~\ref{sec:value} gives broader greedy-recovery and oracle-inequality statements.
